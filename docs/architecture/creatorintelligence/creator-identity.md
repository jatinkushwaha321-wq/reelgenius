# Creator Identity

> Status: Draft v1.0
> Owner: NIVO Creator Intelligence
> Used By: Reasoning Engine V2, Evaluator, Memory

---

# Purpose

Creator Identity is the persistent cognitive representation of a creator within NIVO.

It captures the stable characteristics that define how the creator thinks, what they believe, who they serve, and the transformation they consistently create.

Creator Identity is intentionally independent of individual posts, temporary trends, and platform-specific behavior.

Its purpose is to provide a stable reasoning foundation that remains valid across many observation cycles.

---

# Why Creator Identity Exists

Observed content changes.

Signals evolve.

Audience interests shift.

Trending topics disappear.

Identity should remain comparatively stable.

Without a persistent identity layer, reasoning becomes topic-driven rather than principle-driven.

The Identity Layer ensures that every downstream reasoning process begins with a consistent understanding of who the creator is.

---

# Design Philosophy

Identity is not built from a single post.

Identity emerges from repeated evidence collected over time.

Identity changes slowly.

Reasoning changes every execution.

Ideas change every generation.

---

# Identity Components

## Core Identity

Defines the creator's long-term mission.

Examples:

- Who they help
- What transformation they create
- Why they exist

---

## Audience Model

Represents the audience psychologically.

Includes:

- fears
- aspirations
- frustrations
- motivations
- desired identity

Demographics are secondary.

---

## Core Beliefs

Persistent principles that repeatedly appear across content.

Examples:

- Skills over credentials
- Execution over consumption
- Proof of work matters

Beliefs should be durable.

---

## Identity Invariants

Principles that should rarely change.

These define the creator regardless of trends or platforms.

Examples:

- Encourages execution
- Simplifies complexity
- Promotes independence

---

## Decision Filters

Implicit questions the creator appears to ask before publishing.

Examples:

- Does this help my audience?
- Does this reduce uncertainty?
- Does this encourage action?

---

## Communication Style

Defines how knowledge is delivered.

Includes:

- explanation style
- storytelling
- framework usage
- emotional tone

---

## Vocabulary

Repeated language naturally associated with the creator.

Vocabulary should emerge from evidence rather than manual curation.

---

## Generation Constraints

Defines what content would weaken creator positioning.

Examples:

- Generic AI tool lists
- Generic productivity advice
- Resume optimisation

These constraints are treated as strategic boundaries rather than prompt instructions.

---

# Persistence Model

Creator Identity is persistent.

It survives:

- reasoning executions
- idea generation
- onboarding sessions

Identity evolves only when sufficient new evidence justifies revision.

Frequent identity changes indicate instability rather than learning.

---

# Relationship With Other Systems

## Signal Engine

Provides evidence.

Identity interprets evidence.

---

## Memory

Provides historical context.

Identity provides long-term consistency.

---

## Reasoning Engine

Consumes identity.

Identity does not perform reasoning.

---

## Evaluator

Uses identity as the reference standard.

Generated ideas are evaluated against identity.

---

# Identity Evolution

Identity should evolve conservatively.

Potential triggers include:

- sustained content shifts
- repeated new beliefs
- long-term audience changes
- strategic repositioning

Short-term trends should not alter identity.

---

# Non-Goals

Creator Identity does not:

- store individual posts
- rank signals
- generate ideas
- evaluate quality
- optimise prompts

It exists solely to represent persistent creator understanding.

---

# Success Criteria

The Identity Layer is successful when:

- reasoning consistently aligns with creator philosophy

- generated ideas strengthen creator positioning

- identity remains stable despite temporary trends

- new observations refine identity without causing unnecessary drift

- creators remain distinguishable even when discussing similar topics

The objective is not to describe a creator.

The objective is to preserve the creator's long-term cognitive identity.
